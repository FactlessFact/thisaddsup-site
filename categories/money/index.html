How loan interest works

Loan interest is the extra money you pay when you borrow money from a bank. When you take out a loan, you agree to pay back the amount you borrowed plus interest. This interest is usually a percentage of the loan amount. For example, if you borrow $100, you might pay back $110. Interest can also work the other way. When you save money in a bank, you earn interest too. The bank pays you extra money for keeping your savings there. How does inflation work?

Inflation is when prices for goods and services rise. This means that money buys less than before. We measure inflation with a number called the consumer price index (CPI). When inflation goes up, people may spend less because they worry about prices rising more. Some reasons for inflation include changes in how much people want to buy and changes in supply. While inflation can cause problems, like making it hard to plan for the future, it can also help the economy by lowering unemployment. Most experts agree that a small, steady amount of inflation is best for the economy. ...

The way interest is added can change. It can happen yearly, monthly, or even daily. Many banks show how much compound interest you can earn to help you understand your money better.
